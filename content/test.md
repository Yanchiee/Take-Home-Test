# Take-Home Test: AI Tool Researcher

## Overview

This take-home exercise is designed to evaluate how you **discover, evaluate, and apply AI tools** to real-world business problems.

The goal is not just to arrive at good answers, but to demonstrate:

- How you **find and select AI tools**
- How you **extract non-obvious information**
- How you **cross-reference sources**
- How you **evaluate cost vs. utility**
- How well you **internalize and communicate insights**

You will use a variety of AI tools to research and propose **mobile app business opportunities** that are already generating revenue.

## The Challenge

You will use AI tools to **identify, analyze, and propose three mobile app ideas**, based on real, revenue-generating apps currently in the market.

Your task is to:

1. Identify apps with **published or inferred MRR/ARR**
2. Estimate the **revenue opportunity**
3. Present your findings clearly in both **written** and **video** form
4. You will then come up with your own idea for a new mobile app to develop and launch.

## Key Constraints & Expectations

### 1. Tool Usage (Critical)

You must use **multiple AI tools** to complete this task.

Examples (not exhaustive):

- ChatGPT
- Gemini
- Perplexity
- Any other AI tools you discover

**Important:** Many of the insights you need **will not be found via Google SERP alone**.

You are expected to:

- Go beyond standard Google search results
- Extract information from **non-SERP platforms**, such as:
  - Reddit
  - Twitter / X
  - TikTok
  - Indie hacker communities
  - Founder posts
  - App analytics platforms (e.g., Sensor Tower or equivalents)

In your Loom video, you must **explicitly state**:

- Which tools allowed you to search or surface **non-Google information**
- How those tools differed in capability
- Why you chose certain tools over others

Simply running "deep research" that pulls only Google results will **not be considered sufficient**.

### 2. App Research Requirements

You must analyze and download 3 existing apps. For each app you analyze, you should:

- **Download and use the app**
- Review:
  - Onboarding experience
  - Core value proposition
  - Monetization model and paywalls
  - What makes the app successful or "sticky"
- Identify:
  - Reported or inferred MRR/ARR
  - Primary customer segment
  - Why the app is viable in the **US iOS App Store market**
  - Whether the market is **growing**

### 3. New App Ideas

You will present **a new app idea**, and you may discuss up to three new app ideas:

1. **Primary / Best Idea**
2. Secondary Idea
3. Tertiary Idea

For each idea, explain:

- What the app does
- Why it is valuable
- How it compares to existing revenue-generating apps
- If it could be built quickly by one engineer using Claude Code
- The estimated revenue potential based on competitors

Then return here, click **Finish Test**, and paste that same Drive folder link (the one we provided you by email) into the submission form. That single link is all we need.

## Deliverables

### 1. Written Research Report

Submit a **Google Doc, Word Doc, or PDF** that includes:

- A list of all AI tools used
- Why you chose each tool
- Sources of information (including non-Google platforms)
- App research findings
- Revenue estimates and assumptions
- Your one to three proposed app ideas, clearly labeled

### 1a. A List of All LLM Prompts That You Used

It is crucial during your research that you talk to LLMs, absorb what they are telling you, and then ask better questions. Record a list of all of the questions that you asked to the LLM during your research and submit them in a separate document.

### 2. Loom Video Presentation

Submit a **Loom video** walking through your work. Please use your camera when recording.

**Important rules for the Loom:**

- **Do not read your research report**
- Speak naturally and explain your thinking
- Demonstrate that you have internalized the information

Your Loom should include:

- Explanation of:
  - Which AI tools you used
  - What each tool was best at
  - Which tools surfaced non-SERP data
- How you found revenue data
- **A screenshare of your phone** so that you can demo your research apps
- Why your **top idea** is the strongest opportunity

#### Screen Sharing Requirements

- Share your **computer screen**
- Also share your **phone screen** (mirrored to your computer)
  - Show the actual apps you researched
  - Walk through onboarding, paywalls, and key flows
  - Demonstrate what makes them compelling

## Evaluation Criteria

You will be evaluated on:

- **Depth of research**
- **Quality of tool selection**
- **Ability to find non-obvious information**
- **Critical thinking and synthesis**
- **Cost vs. utility analysis of tools**
- **Clarity of communication**
- **Strength and realism of app ideas**

There is no single "correct" answer. We are evaluating **how you think, research, and communicate**, not just the final ideas.

## Final Notes

This role is about **leverage** — finding the right tools, extracting signal from noise, and turning insight into action.

We are not looking for perfection. We are looking for **resourcefulness, curiosity, and clarity of thought**.

Good luck, and we look forward to seeing how you approach the problem.
